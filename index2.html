Eiffel Tower,paris

The Eiffel Tower is a world-famous iron structure in Paris, built in 1889 by engineer Gustave Eiffel for the World's Fair. It is 330 meters tall and was once the tallest structure in the world. The tower has three levels for visitors, offering restaurants, shops, and breathtaking views of Paris. At night, it sparkles with thousands of lights, making it a symbol of romance and French culture.

Tajmahal,India

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent white marble mausoleum in Agra, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it is famous for its beautiful symmetry, large dome, and peaceful garden. As a symbol of love and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, it attracts millions of tourists from around the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
